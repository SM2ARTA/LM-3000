Transactions
TRANSACTIONS

Four Transaction Types

Every physical movement or status change must be one of four types. Each generates specific documents. All registered in SM2ARTA — this is the law.

1. Inbound

Receiving goods INTO a supply chain node

📄 Generates ASN (Advanced Shipping Notification)
WMS creates Receiving Order from ASN
Warehouse executes receiving & files OS&D
Bob receives Receipt Confirmation PDF and checks line by line

2. Moving

Transfer BETWEEN supply chain nodes

📄 Generates OOR at dispatch site (ship out)
📄 Generates ASN at receiving site (ship in)
Bob's big job: chase the receiver to confirm delivery
Complete transaction only after confirmation

3. Outbound

Goods EXIT the supply chain network

📄 Generates OOR (Outbound Order Request)
WMS creates Shipping Order from OOR
Items leave supply chain custody
Venue deployment, returns to supplier, disposal

4. Damage Report

Nomenclature transformation to damaged category

📄 Part of OS&D report at receiving
Original SKU qty reduced, Damaged SKU qty increased
Item stays at same node — just reclassified
Planner decides: reorder, reallocate, or accept

Transaction States: Created → Completed

🔒 CREATED
Reserves stock so it cannot be double-booked. Items are earmarked but haven't physically moved yet. Think of it as "dibs."
✅ COMPLETED
Moves stock between nodes. Items have physically moved and been confirmed received. Inventory balances update.
Bob's analogy: "Created is like putting a 'SOLD' sticker on a couch at a furniture store. It's not in your living room yet, but nobody else can buy it. Completed is when the delivery truck drops it off and you sign for it. Until then, the store still has your couch — you just have dibs."

SM2ARTA is the system of record for all FF&E assets. Every nomenclature, transaction, and inventory balance must be registered here. If it's not in SM2ARTA, it didn't happen.

Non-negotiable: SM2ARTA must be updated daily. A transaction not logged within 24 hours is a compliance violation. Fill as many fields as possible — documents, photos, WMS order numbers. Your future self will thank you when it's time to reconcile.

Two Types of Receiving

Receiving looks different depending on whether goods come from a supplier or from within the network. Bob's verification process is different for each.
📦

Type 1: From Supplier

Containers & local deliveries arriving at warehouse or venue
Bob's job: verify the supplier delivered what was promised
  • Bob created the ASN in SM2ARTA before arrival
  • Rock-it WMS creates Receiving Order from ASN
  • Warehouse receives goods, counts, files OS&D
  • Receipt Confirmation PDF arrives in Materials Mailbox
  • Bob opens PDF and checks line by line against his SM2ARTA Inbound
  • Discrepancies? Investigate. Damage? File Damage Report.
  • Everything matches? Complete transaction in SM2ARTA.
Also check: open Inbounds without Receipt Confirmation. If the Automatic Receipt PDF didn't arrive on time, Bob proactively asks the warehouse: "Did you receive this?"

LP/LM trucks arriving at warehouses or venues
Bob's big job: chase the receiver to confirm delivery

Staying Sane
STAYING SANE

Take a Deep Breath

30–50 active transactions per week. 100+ trucks in your region. 400+ across the network. Three systems. One shared mailbox. It's a lot. Here's how Bob stays on top of it.

Think Air Traffic Control

Your job is closer to ATC than you think
  • Air traffic controllers manage dozens of planes simultaneously
  • They don't memorize everything — they follow procedures
  • They have checklists for every phase of flight
  • They have a co-pilot who double-checks everything
  • And when something goes wrong? They don't panic. They follow the checklist.

Your Co-Pilot: Fellow Bobs

There are three regional planners. You are not alone.

  • Ask a fellow Bob to double-check your work — not because you're bad at your job, but because the job is hard
  • Two pairs of eyes on a 24-pallet Receipt PDF catch what one pair misses
  • Materials Mailbox lets any Bob pick up any thread
  • Color-code your categories so fellow Bobs know what's yours

When It Feels Overwhelming — 5 Steps

1
Pause. Take a breath. Seriously. Five seconds.
2
What zone am I in? Frozen = react. Slushy = manage. Forecast = plan.
3
What's the oldest open transaction? Start there. Complete it.
4
Update SM2ARTA first. Even when the building is on fire. Especially then.
5
Ask for help. Fellow Bob, LCC, your manager. Nobody wins alone.
It may seem like SM2ARTA is an unnecessary step when you're drowning in urgent issues. "I'll update it later." Don't. SM2ARTA is not overhead — it's your lifeline. When someone asks "where are the 48 chairs?" at 4:45 PM on a Friday, the planner with up-to-date SM2ARTA says "let me check" and has the answer in 10 seconds. The planner without it says "let me dig through my emails" and ruins everyone's weekend.
Every mistake can be fixed. Everyone makes them. A wrong quantity, a missed OS&D, a late transaction — these happen. What matters is that you catch it, correct it, and document the correction. The mistake isn't the problem. The unrecorded mistake is.
Bob's wisdom: "On my worst day I had 47 open transactions, 12 unanswered confirmation emails, a container arriving with wrong paperwork, and Rock-it calling about a misrouted pallet. I wanted to throw my laptop out the window. Instead, I made a coffee, opened SM2ARTA, sorted by oldest Created date, and started completing them one by one. By 5 PM I was down to 8. The next morning, my fellow Bob caught a mistake in one of my completed transactions. He fixed it. That's why we're a team."

ONBOARDING

Bob's First Day — Frequently Asked Questions

You've seen the presentation. You have questions. Everyone does. Here are the answers so you can stop worrying and start working.

Systems & Access

"How do I get SM2ARTA access?"Already done. There's a test server to practice without touching real data. Use it. Break things. Learn.
"Do I have access to Rock-it's WMS?"Yes. You can view inventory at Regional/Satellite WHS and see the list of Receiving/Shipping Orders.
"What about ML3K?"View-only access. Click any button guiltlessly — you can't break anything. Refer to the Help section inside ML3K for guidance.
"Materials Mailbox setup?"Access to materials@fwc2026.org is preset. Folder structure is pre-created. Add your Outlook Category with your name.

Day-to-Day Operations & Scope

"How do I create my first ASN/OOR?"Your fellow Bobs will walk you through the first one. After that, you'll do it on your own. It's straightforward once you've seen it.
"How do I know what LP trucks are coming to my region?"Liaise with Regional Distribution Team for truck status. Monitor Materials Mailbox for automatic Receipt Confirmations.
"What if I find a discrepancy in the Receipt PDF?"Complete the transaction with the physical qty actually received. Create a new transaction for the pending/missing items. Investigate what happened.
"Damaged item naming convention?"Add -DMG suffix to the original SKU. Example: CHAIR-001 → CHAIR-001-DMG
"Do I deal with customs?"No. You're part of Events Logistics. The Customs Workstream handles that. Your teammates deal with CI/HS codes.
"What happens after the tournament?"Dissolution — the reverse logistics plan. Details are TBC. For now, focus on getting items to venues.
"Reorder vs accept a shortage?"Escalate to your manager. You flag the damage and impact; they decide the response.

SUMMARY

Key Rules & Quick Reference

These rules are non-negotiable. Print this slide and pin it to your monitor.

The 8 Golden Rules

1 Update SM2ARTA daily. No exceptions. No backlog. This is the law.
2 Always CC materials@fwc2026.org. Your Sent folder is invisible to others.
3 Check Receipt PDFs line by line. Never assume it's correct.
4 Respect the zones. Frozen = execute. Slushy = justify. Forecast = optimize.
5 Every movement is a transaction. Created (reserves) then Completed (moves).
6 OOR to ship, ASN to receive. Moving = both. Always.
7 OS&D when something is wrong. Over, short, or damaged. Photograph first.
8 Ask a fellow Bob to check your work. Not because you're useless — because the job is hard.

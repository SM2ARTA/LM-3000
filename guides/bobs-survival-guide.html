Chapter 2PLANNING ZONES

Three Zones — The Planning Horizon

Like driving: far ahead, change lanes freely. Closer, signal and check mirrors. At the intersection? Committed — just execute.

🌱 FORECAST7+ Days Out

The Highway: Full replanning allowed. Change routes, dates, truck contents. This is where Bob thinks ahead and prevents crises.

DO: Plan, optimize, change anything | DON'T: Ignore it because "it's far away"
💧 SLUSHY72h – 7 Days

The On-Ramp: Confirm forecasts with stakeholders. Venues confirm dates. Distribution pre-books trucks. WHS starts assembly. Changes possible but need justification.

DO: Confirm, communicate, align | DON'T: Make silent changes
❄️ FROZEN0 – 72 Hours

The Intersection: Execute only. OORs submitted, trucks booked, WHS picking. No content changes. Confirm receivings, file OS&D, check Receipt PDFs. Issues? Escalate to LCC — don't replan.

DO: Execute, confirm, escalate | DON'T: Replan, change truck content, panic
💡 Analogy: Restaurant Kitchen
Forecast = Menu planning (change anything). Slushy = Prep cooking (ingredients bought — changes are expensive). Frozen = Plating & serving (the steak is on the plate — you can't un-cook it).
The 72-Hour Rule: Every OOR must be submitted 72+ hours before dispatch. No exceptions. The warehouse needs time to pick, pack, and load.

Chapter 3TRANSACTIONS

Four Transactions — The Building Blocks

Every physical movement = a transaction. Like bank transfers: items move between nodes, SM²ARTA records every penny.

📥 INBOUND

Goods coming INTO a node. Like a deposit into your bank account.

Doc: ASN → Receiving Order → Receipt PDF

🚚 MOVING

Transfer BETWEEN nodes. Like transferring money between accounts. Always OOR + ASN.

Doc: OOR (origin) + ASN (destination)

📤 OUTBOUND

Goods EXIT the network. Like withdrawing cash. Returns, deployment, disposal.

Doc: OOR → Shipping Order

⚠️ DAMAGE

Nomenclature TRANSFORM. Item stays but gets reclassified. SKU → SKU-DMG

Doc: OS&D + Photos + SM²ARTA txn

Transaction States

🔒 CREATED = "Dibs"

Stock reserved but hasn't moved. Like putting a "SOLD" sticker on a couch at IKEA — not in your apartment yet, but nobody else can buy it.

✅ COMPLETED = "Delivered"

Physical transfer confirmed. Balances update. Like when IKEA delivers the couch and you sign for it. Now it's in your living room.

Golden Rule: IN = ASN. OUT = OOR. Moving = BOTH. No document = no movement. Like boarding a plane without a boarding pass — you're not getting on.
💡 Analogy: Online Shopping
Inbound = Amazon package arriving. Moving = Returning to a different locker. Outbound = Donating to Goodwill. Damage = Package arrived crushed — you file a claim but still have the box.
👷
Bob says:"Created is like ordering pizza. The pizza place has your order — they're making it. But it's not at your door. Completed is when you open the box, count the slices, and eat one. If only 7 slices instead of 8? That's an OS&D."

OS&D — When Things Go Wrong

Filed only when something is wrong at receiving. Not routine — it's the exception report.

OVER: Received MORE than expected. "Ordered 8 pizzas, 9 showed up. Free pizza? Still file it."
SHORT: Received LESS. "Ordered 8, got 7. Someone's hungry."
DAMAGE: Items broken. "All 8 arrived but 2 upside down. Toppings everywhere."

Chapter 7RECEIPT PDF

The Receipt PDF — Line by Line

This document lands in Materials Mailbox every time a warehouse receives goods. From Rock-it's WMS (Da Vinci) automatically. Bob's job: don't ignore it.

Bob's 10-Step Checklist

  1. Open the PDF attachment
  2. Check TRANSACTION # — note for SM²ARTA reference
  3. Check RECEIPT DATE — match expected arrival?
  4. Each line: match ITEM CODE to SM²ARTA SKU
  5. Each line: match QTY and UOM to SM²ARTA
  6. Check TOTAL UNITS vs ASN total
  7. Discrepancy? → OS&D investigation
  8. All match? → Complete in SM²ARTA
  9. Attach PDF reference to SM²ARTA transaction
  10. Archive email thread in Materials Mailbox

Chapter 8MAILBOX

Materials Mailbox — Command Center

materials@fwc2026.org is the team's collective brain. Like a shared Google Drive — if the file isn't there, it doesn't exist for the team.

The 5 Commandments

1Always CC the Materials Mailbox. Your Sent folder is invisible to fellow Bobs. Like texting about a meeting but never putting it on the shared calendar.
2Color-code every email with Outlook Category = your name. Like claiming a parking spot with a cone — fellow Bobs know you're on it.
3Archive resolved threads daily. Inbox = active items only. Clean desk policy — if it's done, file it.
4Use existing folder structure. Resolved emails out of inbox by EOD. Like a filing cabinet — everything has a drawer.
5Never delete. Archive only. Emails are evidence. Like a court case — you can't un-shred a document.
💡 Analogy: Hospital Nurse Station
A hospital has a central nurse station where every doctor's note, patient update, and medication change is logged. If Nurse A goes home sick, Nurse B picks up any patient because all notes are at the station — not in Nurse A's personal notebook. Your mailbox IS the nurse station.

When They Don't Reply (Escalation)

+24hFollow-up email (CC Materials Mailbox, always)
+48hTeams message or phone call
+72hEscalate to LCC (Logistics Coordination Center)
Keep all follow-ups in the same email thread. Never start a new thread for the same delivery.

Chapter 10DAILY RHYTHM

Bob's Daily Rhythm

Like a pilot's pre-flight checklist — same sequence, every day, no shortcuts. The routine IS the safety net.

☕ MORNING — Frozen Zone ❄️

  • Open Materials Mailbox — scan for Receipt PDFs and venue confirmations
  • Check line by line: PDF vs SM²ARTA Inbound. Every. Line.
  • Check open Inbounds without Receipt Confirmation — chase WHS
  • Review OS&D reports — any shorts or damage?

  • Check ML3K for upcoming 7-day forecast
  • Create OORs & ASNs for next dispatch window
  • Send venue confirmation emails (CC Materials Mailbox!)
  • Chase receivers for open Moving transactions
  • Coordinate with Rock-it on WHS processing status

🌇 END OF DAY — Forecast & Close 🌱

  • Verify all day's transactions logged in SM²ARTA
  • Archive resolved email threads from Mailbox
  • Review tomorrow's frozen zone — everything ready?
  • Optimize forecast zone in ML3K
  • Reconcile: plan (ML3K) vs actual (SM²ARTA)
Morning priority: SM²ARTA reconciliation and Receipt PDF verification come first. No planning until yesterday's actuals confirmed. Like a pilot — you don't take off until the pre-flight checklist is complete.

SKU Codes & Kits

Format: FN-CHA-002 → Category (FN/OS/FE) + Type (CHA/TBL) + Number (001...). Kits: OSKIT (office supplies) / FNKIT (tools/fixtures)

UoM (Unit of Measurement)

UoMMeaningExample
EachIndividual item1 chair = 1 EA
BoxBox of items1 box of markers
KitPre-packed kit1 OSKIT = ~14 items
PackMulti-pack1 pack batteries = 10 EA
PairTwo items1 pair of gloves
PPU: Pieces Per Unit. System auto-converts (e.g., 50 batteries ÷ 10/pack = 5 packs).
